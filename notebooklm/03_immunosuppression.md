# Thai Transplantation Care (TTC): Clinical and Legal Framework for Kidney Transplantation

This briefing document synthesizes the clinical standards and legal requirements for kidney transplantation in Thailand, primarily based on the **Thai Transplant Care (TTC 2)** guidelines. It outlines the protocols established by the Thai Transplantation Society in collaboration with the Thai Red Cross Organ Donation Center and the Ministry of Public Health.

## Executive Summary

The Thai transplant landscape is governed by stringent legal and clinical protocols designed to maximize recipient outcomes while ensuring the highest safety and ethical standards for living donors. Current practice emphasizes a standardized evaluation process (TTC 2) to address the national shortage of deceased donors by optimizing living donor (LD) programs. Key pillars of the Thai position include:
*   **Legal Relationship Verification:** Strict definitions of kinship and marital status to prevent commercialization.
*   **Clinical Rigor:** Adoption of international standards (KDIGO and UK Guidelines) adapted for the Thai population, particularly regarding GFR estimation and proteinuria thresholds.
*   **Administrative Oversight:** Centralized registration through the Thai Transplant Registry and oversight by the Thai Red Cross.

## Thai Practice Patterns and Legal Framework

### Legal and Ethical Requirements for Donation
The Thai Transplantation Society enforces specific ethical codes to ensure all living donations are altruistic. These are derived from the Medical Council of Thailand’s regulations (B.E. 2549 and 2553).

*   **Kinship and Biological Ties:** Donors must be genetically related to the recipient. This includes parents, children, and siblings. For extended family (uncles, aunts, cousins, or half-siblings), scientific proof such as DNA or HLA testing is mandatory to establish the relationship [TTC: Kidney, p. 11].
*   **Spousal Donation:** A spouse may donate if the marriage is legally registered for at least three years prior to the transplant. If the couple has a biological child together, the three-year requirement is waived, provided the child's parentage is verified (via DNA if necessary) [TTC: Kidney, p. 8, 11].
*   **Foreign Nationals:** For cases involving foreign donors or recipients, documents must be certified by the relevant embassy or governmental agency and further validated by the Department of Consular Affairs of Thailand. Genetic relationships must be confirmed by DNA/HLA testing at a Thai government medical institute [TTC: Kidney, p. 14].
*   **Altruism and Compensation:** Thai law strictly prohibits any payment or benefit in exchange for organs. Surgeons must obtain written informed consent confirming that no compensation was provided [TTC: Kidney, p. 11].

### Institutional Infrastructure
Transplantation in Thailand is a coordinated effort involving:
*   **The Thai Red Cross Organ Donation Center:** Manages the allocation and registration of member hospitals.
*   **Thai Transplantation Society:** Establishes clinical guidelines and professional standards.
*   **The Thai Transplant Registry:** Maintains comprehensive data on transplant outcomes [TTC: Kidney, p. 2, 23].

## Clinical Evaluation and Monitoring Protocols

Thai protocols for donor evaluation are exhaustive, mirroring high-level international standards to ensure long-term donor safety.

### Quantitative Acceptance Criteria (Fellow-Grade Analysis)

| Parameter | Threshold / Target | Thai TTC 2 Position / Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Donor Age** | No absolute upper limit | Evaluated on a case-by-case basis. Donors >60 years are accepted but require counseling regarding age-related GFR decline and potential for shorter graft survival [TTC: Kidney, p. 23-24]. |
| **Ideal GFR** | >90 mL/min/1.73 m² | Recommended for all donors. Calculated via **CKD-EPI 2009** for initial screening [TTC: Kidney, p. 27]. |
| **Borderline GFR**| 70–89 mL/min/1.73 m² | Accepted after thorough counseling regarding post-donation risks, factoring in age and comorbidities [TTC: Kidney, p. 27]. |
| **Excluded GFR** | <70 mL/min/1.73 m² | Mandatory exclusion from the donation program [TTC: Kidney, p. 27]. |
| **Proteinuria** | AER >100 mg/day | Donors with an Albumin Excretion Rate (AER) >100 mg/day or Protein Excretion Rate >300 mg/day are excluded [TTC: Kidney, p. 29]. |
| **Hematuria** | ≥3–5 RBC/HPF | Considered microscopic hematuria; requires culture to rule out UTI and imaging to rule out nephrolithiasis or malignancy [TTC: Kidney, p. 31]. |

### Monitoring and Screening Schedule
Potential donors undergo a multi-phasic screening process:
1.  **Initial Triage (2–4 weeks):** ABO/HLA compatibility, medical history, and routine blood/urinalysis [SOURCE_IMAGE_2].
2.  **Comprehensive Assessment (Further 4 weeks):** Consultant-led service using "gold standard" investigative procedures [SOURCE_IMAGE_2].
3.  **Specific Laboratory Screening:**
    *   **Metabolic:** Fasting plasma glucose (GTT required if FPG >5.6 mmol/L), lipid profile, and urate [TTC: Kidney, p. 21].
    *   **Virology:** Hepatitis B/C, HIV, HTLV 1/2, CMV, EBV, Toxoplasma, and Syphilis [TTC: Kidney, p. 21].
    *   **Cardiovascular:** ECG and Chest X-ray (standard); ECHO or stress tests (indicated for older donors or those with risk factors) [TTC: Kidney, p. 22].

## Technical Insights: GFR and Proteinuria Estimation

The TTC 2 guidelines provide specific technical mandates for the measurement of renal function in the Thai population:

*   **Initial eGFR Calculation:** The **2009 CKD-EPI** equation is the standard for initial screening.
*   **Confirmatory Measured GFR (mGFR):** If eGFR is borderline or accuracy is questioned, the guidelines recommend exogenous markers including:
    *   Inulin (urinary or plasma clearance).
    *   Iothalamate.
    *   51Cr-EDTA or Iohexol.
    *   99mTc-DTPA (urinary clearance) [TTC: Kidney, p. 26].
*   **Individual Kidney GFR:** If kidneys differ in size by >10% or have anatomical abnormalities, a radionuclide scan (e.g., 99mTc-DTPA) must be performed to ensure the smaller or less functional kidney is donated, leaving the donor with the more robust organ [TTC: Kidney, p. 27-28].
*   **Proteinuria Preferred Metrics:** The guidelines favor the **Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (ACR)** over standard urine protein-to-creatinine ratios (UPCR) for higher sensitivity [TTC: Kidney, p. 29].

## Important Quotes and Contextual Commentary

> "The shortage of kidney donors... is an important problem in the transplantation process in Thailand... it is an urgent matter for the Transplantation Society, the Ministry of Public Health, and the Red Cross to push policies regarding organ donation."
> — **Assoc. Prof. Dr. Kriangsak Varie-sangthip, President of the Thai Transplantation Society [TTC: Kidney, p. 2]**

*   **Context:** This highlights the motivation behind the TTC 2 guidelines: creating a streamlined, legally sound pathway for living donation to mitigate the lack of deceased donors.

> "A donor must be a genetic relative... or a husband/wife by law... living together openly for at least three years."
> — **Medical Council Regulation on Organ Transplantation [TTC: Kidney, p. 8]**

*   **Context:** This underscores the Thai legal stance against "transplant tourism" and commercial organ trafficking by requiring documented proof of long-term social or biological bonds.

## Actionable Insights for Transplant Programs

*   **Pre-emptive Planning:** Establish recipient fitness and initiate donor assessment within 2 weeks of referral to promote pre-emptive living donor transplantation as the treatment of choice [SOURCE_IMAGE_2].
*   **Donor Safety Margin:** When facing donors with disparate kidney function (>10% difference), the surgical plan must prioritize the donor's long-term health by retaining the kidney with the higher GFR [TTC: Kidney, p. 27-28].
*   **Spousal Verification:** Programs must ensure rigorous documentation of marital status, including 24-hour access to legal registries or DNA parentage testing for children of the union to prevent delays in the three-year waiting period waiver [TTC: Kidney, p. 8].
*   **Long-term Follow-up:** The Living Donor (LD) Coordinator must facilitate life-long follow-up arrangements to provide continuing support and inform the national registry of long-term donor outcomes [SOURCE_IMAGE_3].
