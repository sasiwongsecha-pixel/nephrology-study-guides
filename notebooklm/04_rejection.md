# Living Kidney Donor Evaluation and Transplantation Care (TTC 2)

## Executive Summary
This briefing document synthesizes the clinical guidelines and ethical frameworks established by the Thai Transplantation Society in the **Thai Transplant Care (TTC 2)** manual (September 2016). The primary objective of these guidelines is to ensure the highest standard of care for living kidney donors and recipients in Thailand, prioritizing donor safety and ethical integrity. The document outlines a comprehensive medical evaluation protocol, encompassing legal requirements for donation, standardized glomerular filtration rate (GFR) assessment, and strict criteria for proteinuria and hematuria. By adhering to international evidence-based standards (including references to UK guidelines and KDIGO), the protocol seeks to maximize the benefits of transplantation while minimizing surgical and long-term health risks to the donor.

## Detailed Analysis of Key Themes

### 1. Legal and Ethical Framework for Living Donation
The source emphasizes that living kidney donation must strictly adhere to the ethical and legal standards set by the Medical Council of Thailand (2010) and the Thai Red Cross Organ Donation Centre. The core principle is the prevention of organ trafficking and the assurance of altruistic intent.

*   **Relationship Requirements:** Donors must be either genetically related or legally recognized spouses. 
    *   **Genetic Relations:** Includes parents, biological children, and siblings. Extended relations (uncles, aunts, cousins) are permitted if biological relationship is proven via DNA or HLA testing.
    *   **Spousal Relations:** Must be legally married or in an open relationship for at least three years. This duration requirement is waived if the couple has biological children together, though DNA proof may be required in cases of doubt.
*   **Informed Consent:** Physicians are mandated to explain all surgical risks and potential long-term health impacts. Donors must sign an informed consent form and have the right to withdraw at any time prior to surgery.
*   **Non-Commercialization:** Documentation must prove that no financial compensation or benefits were provided to the donor in exchange for the organ.

### 2. Standardized Medical Evaluation of Renal Function (GFR)
A central theme of the TTC 2 guidelines is the move toward standardized GFR measurement rather than relying solely on serum creatinine levels.

*   **Measurement Protocols:** The guidelines recommend the 2009 CKD-EPI equation for estimating GFR (eGFRcr). If accuracy is questioned, measured GFR (mGFR) using exogenous markers (e.g., Inulin, Iothalamate, or 99mTc-DTPA) or 24-hour creatinine clearance (mCLcr) is required.
*   **Anatomical Considerations:** If imaging (Ultrasound or CT) shows a size difference between kidneys of >10%, individual kidney GFR must be measured. The smaller or less functional kidney should generally be harvested for donation, leaving the more robust kidney with the donor.

### 3. Urinary Marker Assessment: Proteinuria and Hematuria
The presence of protein or blood in the urine serves as a critical screening threshold for donor suitability.

*   **Proteinuria:** The guidelines favor the use of the albumin-to-creatinine ratio (ACR) or protein-to-creatinine ratio (UPCR) from spot urine samples for initial screening. Confirmation is achieved through 24-hour timed urine collection.
*   **Hematuria:** Defined as ≥3–5 red blood cells per high power field (RBC/hpf) in centrifuged urine. Persistent microhematuria requires extensive investigation for stones, infection, or malignancy before donation can be considered.

### 4. Donor Age and Long-term Safety
The guidelines reflect a global shift toward accepting older donors while acknowledging the inherent risks. 
*   **Upper Age Limits:** While many international centers have moved away from strict upper age limits, the Thai guidelines emphasize that older donors (typically >60 years) must undergo rigorous evaluation. 
*   **Outcomes:** Research cited in the source indicates that for donors over 60, operative time, blood loss, and hospital stay duration are comparable to younger cohorts, provided the donor is otherwise healthy.

## Verbatim Scoring and Cutoff Criteria

The following table outlines the standardized cutoffs for donor acceptance based on the TTC 2 clinical guidelines:

| Category | Verbatim Cutoff / Criteria | Action / Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GFR (Acceptable)** | `GFR >90 ml/min/1.73 m2` | Acceptable for donation |
| **GFR (Conditional)** | `70-89 ml/min/1.73 m2` | Acceptable after risk explanation and considering co-morbidities |
| **GFR (Excluded)** | `nâoy wàa 70 ml/min/1.73 m2` (less than 70) | Excluded from donation |
| **Proteinuria (AER)** | `AER mâak kwàa 100 mg/day` (more than 100) | Excluded from donation |
| **Proteinuria (Total)** | `urine protein excretion rate mâak kwàa 300 mg/day` | Excluded from donation |
| **Hematuria** | `≥ 3-5 dtua dtòr high power field` (3-5 cells per HPF) | Defined as hematuria; requires investigation |
| **Kidney Size Diff.** | `kà-nâat dtàang gan > 10%` (size difference > 10%) | Requires individual GFR measurement |
| **Spousal Duration** | `mâi nâoy kwàa 3 bpee` (not less than 3 years) | Required unless the couple has a biological child |

## Important Quotes with Context

### On Ethical Conduct and Penalties
> "When a member hospital is accused of violating ethical standards, it is the duty of said hospital to provide evidence that it has complied with ethical standards. If the hospital cannot provide satisfactory evidence... the subcommittee may consider revoking membership." (**Clause 56-57**)

*Context: This quote emphasizes the "burden of proof" placed on medical institutions to maintain the integrity of the Thai transplant system, specifically regarding the prevention of organ trafficking.*

### On Informed Consent
> "The medical practitioner performing the transplant must explain to the donor the risks of various dangers that may occur to the donor, both from the surgery or after the surgery... Once the donor understands and is willing to donate, they must sign a written informed consent form." (**Clause 52, Item 6**)

*Context: This establishes the legal requirement for donor education, ensuring that donation is truly voluntary and that all potential complications are disclosed.*

### On Spousal Donation
> "The donor must be a spouse with evidence of marriage registration... not less than 3 years, except in cases where there are children together... in cases of doubt regarding children, use DNA as proof." (**Clause 50**)

*Context: This regulation is a safeguard against "marriages of convenience" intended solely for the illegal sale of kidneys.*

## Actionable Insights for Donor Assessment

*   **Standardize Estimation Formulas:** Clinical teams should utilize the **2009 CKD-EPI** equation for initial GFR estimation. If the result falls in the 70–89 range, it must be confirmed with a measured GFR (mGFR) or 24-hour collection before proceeding.
*   **Prioritize Albuminuria over Proteinuria:** Initial screening should utilize **ACR (albumin-to-creatinine ratio)** rather than general protein dipsticks where possible, as it is a more sensitive indicator of early renal damage.
*   **Verify Relationship Status Early:** Before beginning expensive medical evaluations, the legal and genetic relationship between donor and recipient must be verified via official documentation or DNA/HLA testing to ensure compliance with Medical Council regulations.
*   **Adopt the "UK Model" for Timelines:** The guidelines suggest following the UK assessment timeline, aiming for recipient fit assessment within **2 weeks of referral** and identification of potential donors/ABO compatibility within **2–4 weeks**.
*   **Manage Anatomical Discrepancy:** In cases where one kidney is smaller or has anatomical variations, the rule of thumb remains: **"Harvest the lesser, keep the better"** for the donor to ensure their long-term renal reserve.
